Topic

Japanese Food Culture

Situation
You are talking with a foreign coworker or friend about Japanese food and eating habits.

Dialogue

  • A: I’m curious about Japanese food culture. Do people usually eat out or cook at home?
  • B: It depends, but many people cook at home during the week.
  • A: What kind of meals do you usually have?
  • B: Simple dishes like rice, miso soup, and grilled fish are common.
  • A: That sounds healthy. Do you eat together as a family?
  • B: Yes, especially in the evening. It’s an important time to relax and talk.

Key Phrases You Can Reuse

It depends, but ~
→ はっきり言い切らずに説明するときの便利表現

Many people ~
→ 日本の一般的な習慣を説明するときに使える

~ are common.
→ よくあること・一般的なことを伝える表現

It’s an important time to ~
→ 文化的な価値を説明するときに使える

Focus
Sound linking in natural speech

Common Patterns

  • want to → wanna
  • going to → gonna

Practice: Talk About Yourself

Answer the questions in your own words.

  1. Do you usually cook at home or eat out?
  2. What kind of Japanese food do you like the most?
  3. Is eating together important in your family? Why?

Model Answer（例）
I usually cook at home on weekdays because it’s healthier and cheaper.
My favorite Japanese food is ramen because it’s comforting and easy to enjoy.
Eating together is important in my family because we can relax and talk about our day.

Phrase Focus

  1. Explaining Japanese culture in simple English
  2. Talking about habits and daily life
  3. Sharing personal opinions and experiences
  4. Speaking clearly without using difficult words
