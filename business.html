Lesson: Business English You Can Use at Work

Situation
Checking project progress with a colleague at work

Dialogue

  • A: Hi, do you have a moment?
  • B: Sure. What’s up?
  • A: I wanted to check on the progress of the proposal.
  • B: It’s almost done. I’m just making a few final adjustments.
  • A: Great. When do you think it will be ready?
  • B: I should be able to send it to you by tomorrow morning.
  • A: That works for me. Thanks for the update.
  • B: Of course. I’ll keep you posted.

Key Phrases You’ll Use All the Time

Do you have a moment?
→ A polite way to start a work-related conversation

I wanted to check on ~
→ Natural and professional way to ask about progress

It’s almost done.
→ Common phrase to say something is nearly finished

I’m just making a few final adjustments.
→ Polite explanation when work is not fully complete

When do you think it will be ready?
→ Standard way to ask about deadlines

That works for me.
→ Professional and friendly agreement

I’ll keep you posted.
→ Very common business closing phrase

Phrase Focus

  1. Polite ways to start a business conversation
  2. Asking about progress without sounding pushy
  3. Talking about deadlines professionally
  4. Closing conversations in a calm, confident way

Practice Tip

Goal: Speak without stopping

  1. Read the dialogue using a calm, professional tone
  2. Shadow Speaker A for question patterns
  3. Replace proposal / tomorrow morning with your real work tasks
